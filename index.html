My Approach

Working with the mind,
the body, and what lies beneath.

My work integrates psychotherapy, trauma-informed care, somatic awareness and subconscious healing. We explore patterns gently while also paying attention to how they live in the body and in everyday life.

Alongside psychological and body-based work, I also remain open to the deeper inner questions many people carry — around meaning, identity and a sense of connection with themselves.

For some, this becomes an important part of the healing process.

When it does, we explore it in a grounded and thoughtful way, always anchored in emotional safety and clarity.

Services

Every person's process is different. We decide together.

Individual Therapy

A space to understand yourself more clearly and work through patterns that affect your day-to-day life.

  • Inner child work to process early experiences and parental dynamics
  • Relationship challenges, including attachment patterns and communication
  • Anxiety, overthinking and emotional overwhelm
  • Self-worth, identity and confidence
  • Navigating life transitions and decision-making
Paced carefully so you feel supported as you explore and shift these patterns.

Trauma-Focused Therapy

Understanding and processing past experiences, reducing triggers, building safety.

  • Processing childhood trauma, emotional neglect, or criticism
  • Understanding triggers and reducing emotional reactivity
  • Working through shame, fear, or chronic self-doubt
  • Healing patterns of hypervigilance, freeze, or shutdown
  • Rebuilding a sense of safety within yourself
Gradual and always guided by your capacity.

Somatic Work

Working with the body to release stress, regulate emotions and build internal safety.

  • Noticing and understanding body-based responses (tightness, breath, shutdown)
  • Learning to regulate your nervous system in moments of stress
  • Releasing stored tension and survival responses
  • Building a greater sense of safety and presence in your body
  • Reducing anxiety that feels physical or immediate
Helps shift patterns that talking alone often cannot reach.

Hypnotherapy

Subconscious work for deeper patterns, beliefs, and emotional responses.

  • Identifying and shifting core beliefs about yourself
  • Working with repeating relationship patterns
  • Addressing performance anxiety or confidence blocks
  • Processing emotional memories linked to past experiences
  • Working through sexual blocks or body-based inhibitions
You remain aware and in control throughout the process.

Self Integration Work (Shadow Work)

Working with different parts of self to create internal coherence and stability.

  • Working with conflicting parts (e.g. one part wants closeness, another pulls away)
  • Understanding protective patterns like perfectionism or people-pleasing
  • Reconnecting with younger or vulnerable parts of yourself
  • Reducing inner conflict and self-criticism
  • Building a more stable and cohesive sense of self
Over time, this creates a deeper sense of internal steadiness.

Mindset & Personal Growth

For those who feel stuck in their thinking, direction or decision-making.

  • Identifying and shifting limiting beliefs
  • Working through imposter syndrome and self-doubt
  • Improving clarity in career or academic decisions
  • Building focus, motivation, and consistency
  • Developing a healthier relationship with failure and growth
More forward-focused, while still grounded in self-awareness.

Start Here

You don't need to be in a crisis to begin therapy.

Most people start because something doesn't feel quite right. You may feel stuck in certain patterns, overwhelmed more often than you'd like, or simply sense that the way you've been coping is no longer working. You don't have to have a clear reason or the right words. Starting is enough.

01

What it's like to begin

It's normal to feel unsure — will I know what to say, will this help, am I overthinking this. These questions are part of the process. The first session is simply a conversation: we take time to understand what's bringing you in and what you're hoping for. There's no pressure to go deeper than you're ready for.

02

A note on discomfort

Therapy can feel uncomfortable at times. You may find yourself noticing things you've avoided or sitting with unfamiliar emotions. That discomfort isn't the goal — it's part of making space for something new. Over time, therapy helps you understand your patterns, feel more steady, and build a more supportive relationship with yourself.

Who this work may not be for: This work may not be the right fit if you are currently experiencing a severe psychiatric crisis that requires medical or hospital-based care, or if you are seeking diagnosis or medication management, which is best handled by a psychiatrist. If you're unsure whether this approach is right for you, we can explore that together during an initial consultation.

Still deciding? A short intro call can help you figure out if this is the right fit — no commitment needed.
